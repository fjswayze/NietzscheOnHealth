Food

On English Cuisine

"But English diet, too- which is, compared to the German and even to the French, a kind of ‘return to nature,” meaning to cannibalizm - is profoundly at odds with my instinct: it seems to that it gives the spirit heavy feet."

On German Cuisine

"But German cuisine quite generally - what doesn’t it have on its conscience! Soup before the meal... overcooked meats, vegetables cooked with fat and flour; the degeneration of pastries and puddings into paperweights!"

On Food and the German Spirit

"The German spirit is an indigestion: it does not finish with anything."

On Meal Timing and Size

"A hearty meal is easier to digest than one that is too small....One has to know the size of one’s stomach... no meals between meals."

On Climate

"Sit as little as possible; give no credence to any thought that was not born outdoors while one moved about freely -- in which the muscles are not celebrating a feast, too. All prejudices come from the intestines."

"The sedentary life - as I have said once before - is the real sin against the holy spirit."

"The slightest sluggishness of the intestines is entirely sufficient, once it has become a bad habit, to turn a geniusinto something mediocre, something ‘German’. The German climate alone is enough to discourage strong, even inherently heroic, intestines."

Drinks

"Alcohol is bad for me: a single glass of wine or beer in one day is quite sufficient to turn my life into a value of misery… I cannot advise all more spiritual natures earnestly enough to abstain entirely from alcohol. Water is sufficient."
"no coffee: coffee spreads darkness"
"Tea is wholesome only in the morning. A little, but strong: tea is very unwholesome and sicklies one o’er the whole day if it is too weak by a single degree."
"In a climate that is very agacant, tea is not advisable for the beginning: one should began an hour earlier with a cup of thick, oil-less cocoa."
